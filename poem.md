# The Long Way Home

The kettle clicks. The window holds
a square of weather, going gold,
and steam ascends in idle scrawl
to write its nothing on the wall.

I came here looking for a door
and found instead the kitchen floor,
the chair, the cup, the quiet hum
of ordinary things that come

unbidden, like the light at four
that lays its hand across the floor
and asks for nothing in return
except that someone watch it burn.

So this is where the journey ends:
not with the trumpets, not with friends,
but with a cup gone slowly cool
and afternoon, that patient pool,

and me, who walked the longer mile
to learn to sit a little while
and let the gold come down the hall —
the long way home was home, after all.
